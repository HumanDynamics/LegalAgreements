ITEM 5.02. COMPENSATORY ARRANGEMENTS OF CERTAIN OFFICERS.

 

On February 8, 2016, the Compensation Committee of the Board of Directors of Gartner, Inc. (the “Company” or “Gartner”) approved the following compensatory arrangements for the Company’s principal executive officer (Eugene A. Hall); principal financial officer (Craig W. Safian); and named executive officers (as most recently set forth in the Company’s 2015 proxy statement dated April 14, 2015) who presently serve as executive officers of the Company (Alwyn Dawkins - senior vice president, events and Per Anders Waern - senior vice president, consulting) (collectively, Messrs. Hall, Safian, Dawkins and Waern are referred to herein as the “Officers”) as follows:

 

2016 Executive Performance Bonus Plan.

 

The Compensation Committee approved and made Gartner’s 2016 Executive Performance Bonus Plan award and established 2016 target bonus amounts that may be earned by Messrs. Hall, Safian, Dawkins and Waern of $953,607; $360,500; $315,982; and $315,982, respectively. These target bonus amounts (100%) will be adjusted from 0% to 200% of target based upon achievement of the performance metrics set by the Compensation Committee relating to 2016 EBITDA (50%) and Total Contract Value (TCV) measured at December 31, 2016 (50%), and certification of such achievement by the Compensation Committee in February 2017. The target amounts represent 105% of base salary for Mr. Hall and 70% of base salary for each of Messrs. Safian, Dawkins and Waern.

 

2016 Long - Term Incentive Award.

 

The Compensation Committee approved and made 2016 annual long-term incentive equity awards to the Officers under our stockholder-approved 2014 Long-Term Incentive Plan consisting of Stock Appreciation Rights (SARs) and Performance Stock Units (PSUs) as follows:

 

Name   Number of SARs   Target Number of PSUs*
Eugene A. Hall   145,703     70,057
Craig Safian   25,977   12,490
Alwyn Dawkins   21,675   10,422
Per Anders Waern   21,675   10,422

 

* Adjusted as described in the narrative below

 

 

Stock Appreciation Rights. The Stock Appreciation Rights Agreements provide for the grant of a number of stock appreciation rights that will be paid in shares of Gartner common stock once the applicable vesting criteria have been met. Assuming continued service through each vesting date, the SARs vest in four equal annual installments commencing February 8, 2017 and expire 7 years from grant. Upon exercise of the SARs, the Officer must pay a purchase price per share equal to the value of Gartner’s common stock on the date of grant ($80.06), or the Officer may settle the SARs by allowing the Company to withhold a sufficient number of shares that is equal in value to the aggregate exercise price. The Company will withhold a portion of the shares subject to the grant to cover applicable tax withholding.

 

Performance Units. The Performance Stock Unit Agreements provide for the grant of a target number of PSUs that will be paid in shares of Gartner common stock once the applicable vesting criteria have been met. The actual number of PSUs granted depends upon achievement of a performance metric set by the Compensation Committee relating to Gartner’s TCV measured at December 31, 2016. The target number of PSUs will be adjusted from 0% and 200% of target based upon achievement of the performance metric, and

 

certification of such achievement by the Compensation Committee in February 2017. Assuming continued service through each vesting date, the PSUs, as adjusted, will vest in four equal annual installments commencing February 8, 2017. The Company will withhold a portion of the shares subject to the grant to cover applicable tax withholding.

 

Stockholder Rights. SAR and PSU recipients do not have stockholder rights, including voting rights and the right to receive dividends and distributions, until after actual shares of Gartner common stock are released upon exercise or vesting.

 

The Forms of 2016 Stock Appreciation Right Agreement and 2016 Performance Stock Unit Agreement are attached hereto as Exhibit 10.1 and Exhibit 10.2, respectively, and are incorporated by reference herein.
